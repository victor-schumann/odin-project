A simple yet delicious dish straight from India: a mild curry with tomato-onion-cream based sauce and boneless chicken pieces carefully cooked. Except it is not realsly from India because I butchered the recipe, but I promise it's tasty.

Ingredients

Seasoning

  • Paprika, 1 tbsp
  • Salt, 1/2 a tbsp
  • Black pepper, infinite¬≤/servings
  • Curry, 2 tbsp
  • Saffron, 1/2 tbsp
  • Cumin, same as black pepper, but a little less
  • Nutmeg, a tiny bit

Actual Chicken

  • Boneless Chicken, 500 g, cubed
  • Butter, 2 tbsp
  • Onions, 2 medium-sized, minced
  • Ginger, a big toe, minced
  • Garlic Gloves, 3, minced
  • Tomato Extract, 2 tbsp
  • Heavy Cream (33%), 200 ml
  • Oil, 2 tbsp
  • Lemon Juice, 1 lemon
  • Coriander/Cilantro, a tiny bunch for aesthetics

Recipe

  1. Dice everything, separate the ingredients and mix evething in the Seasoning section
  2. In a bowl, mix chicken with whatever you like to marinate it. I like using salt, shoyu (JUST A BIT, STOP SCREAMING), black pepper, lemon. Leave it for around an hour in teh fridge.
  3. Roast the chicken in small chunks in a frying pan with a lil butter. It's important to avoind putting a lot so it doesn't let too much water out. Our goal is to make it golden as fast as possible and to put it all in a bowl. Use the same pan you're going to make the dish for extra free time when you're cleaning the mess.
  4. When it's done, put it all back in the frying pan, and (no heat), and put onions in the actual pan to saut√©. When that's done, throw the garlic and the ginger.
  5. For the love of this dish, dont let the garlic burn or the dish will be a lot weirder than it already is. When ready, turn the heat down, throw in a bit, JUST A BIT of the seasoning mix you made, mix for a while and then put in the chicken.
  6. Add butter, cream, about 50% of what's left of the powder mix, tomato puree and pretty much everything else.

Post Script

Now, very important side note: if it is too thick, put milk, if it is too liquid, put tomato puree and/or cream. Use the rest of the powder to balance things a bit too. And if you ahve any of it left, don't store it under the sun/light, hide it where it's dark.
